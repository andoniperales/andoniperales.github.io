A mock-up for a minimalist desktop environment

June 1st, 2019

I just came up with a mock-up for a desktop environment that does away with the idea of windows and tabs, while also rejecting the tiling window manager approach. It is meant to be light, straight-forward, fast, and used exclusively with a keyboard. This is my vision of what a distraction-free, single-tasking environment would look like.

Home

The first thing you see is the essential information: the time, date, and battery and network status —I added them after saving these images, but they would be visible here as well—. No notifications of any kind. The menu's on the left side; pressing any key would automatically trigger it and return a list of your installed programs.

File manager

I've borrowed its general design from ranger, although I went with two columns instead of three, plus the right-hand one is a bit narrower.

Video player

Perhaps a media program—instead of separate video and music programs —would be a better choice; in any case, it would look similar to this one.

Messaging program

A generic messaging program. This could be e-mail, a P2P messaging platform, whatever. Messages are arranged by read and unread. Again, no notifications, red bubbles or other shenanigans. If you've opened it, the message automatically goes to the read folder.
